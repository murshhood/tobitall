NOUN

Non-Countable Noun

The nouns that cannot be counted are called non-countable nouns.

Examples in words:

  1. Water
  2. Sugar
  3. Oil
  4. Salt etc.
You cannot say “1 water, 2 water, 3 water” because water is not countable.

Abstract nouns and proper nouns are always non-countable nouns, but common nouns and concrete nouns can be both count and non-count nouns.

Examples in sentences:

  1. I need some drinking water.
  2. Have some juice, please.
  3. Do you need some gas for your car?
  4. We need a lot of money for the business.
  5. Do you want some milk?
