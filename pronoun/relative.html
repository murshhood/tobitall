Relative Pronoun

Relative pronouns introduce the relative clause. They are used to make clear what is being talked about in a sentence. They describe something more about the subject or the object.

Examples:

  1. The car that was stolen was the one they loved most.
  2. A person who loves nature is a good person.
  3. Our school, which was founded in 1995, is being renovated.
  4. I will accept whichever party dress you buy me on Valentine's Day.
  5. Whoever you are behind this great initiative, I want to thank you.
