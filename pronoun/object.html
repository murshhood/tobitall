PRONOUN

Object Pronoun

Object pronouns work as the object or indirect object in a sentence replacing the antecedent object. This form of the pronoun is also used after prepositions.

Examples:

  1. I’ll give you a present on your birthday. I have a great idea for you. (after preposition)
  2. Tell her that you’ll take the job.li>
  3. I have a gift for your boss. Give it to your boss. (Here, ‘it’works as an object).
  4. Robert trusted him with his life.
  5. She was jealous of him.
