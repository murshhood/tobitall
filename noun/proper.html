NOUN

Proper Noun

A proper noun is a name which refers only to a single person, place, or thing and there is no common name for it. In written English, a proper noun always begins with capital letters.

Examples in words:

  1. Ibadan: (it refers to only one particular city),
  2. Tunde: (refers to a particular person), Australia (there is no other country named Australia; this name is fixed for only one country).

Examples in sentences:

  1. Alex is a wonderful player.
  2. He plays for Sydney Sixers.
  3. He was born in England.
  4. He moved to Australia when he was young.
  5. He lives in Sydney.
