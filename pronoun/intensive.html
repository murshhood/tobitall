PRONOUN

Intensive Pronoun

Intensive pronouns add emphasis/importance but do not act as the object in the sentence. They can appear right after the subject.

Examples:

  1. I will do it myself. (Here, ‘myself’ is not an object)
  2. I myself saw the missing boat into the harbor.
  3. We intend to do all the work ourselves.
  4. You yourselves are responsible for this mess.
